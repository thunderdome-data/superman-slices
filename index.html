When "Man of Steel" hits theaters on June 14, Henry Cavill will join a long list of actors to squeeze into Superman's blue tights. Like all incarnations of the character, "Man of Steel" will be a unique variation of the comic books, cartoons, TV shows and live action films that came before. Here's a look at seven men who became, for a time, "super."

Kirk Alyn

Two movies: 'Superman' and 'Atom Man vs. Superman'

Year(s): 1948 to 1950

A young Superman is sent to Earth by his biological parents and raised as Clark Kent by a foster couple. By day, Clark embarks on a career at the Daily Planet newspaper in the city of Metropolis. Later, in battling the "Spider Lady," Clark saves mankind as the fully realized Superman.

Photo: Houston Chronicle archives

George Reeves

Movie and TV series: 'Superman and the Mole Men' and 'Adventures of Superman'

Year(s): 1951 to 1958

Clark Kent and his Daily Planet colleague Lois Lane travel to a small town to report a story about the world's deepest oil well. Soon after the well’s opening, a race of furry creatures (“Mole Men”) emerges from underground. The creatures inadvertently cause panic throughout the town, and Superman intervenes to wage peace.

Photo: AP

Christopher Reeve

Four movies: 'Superman (I-IV)'

Year(s): 1978 to 1987

Through these four definitive films released during a nine-year span, Superman battles such enemies as Lex Luthor, General Zod, Ursa, Non and Nuclear Man. Superman also fights his dark side, his lust for Lana Lang, and the ability to conceal his super powers from Lois. Christopher Reeve’s portrayal of Superman during this franchise proved to be the most memorable role of his career.

Photo: AP

Dean Cain

TV series: 'Lois & Clark: The New Adventures of Superman'

Year(s): 1993 to 1997

In this ‘90s TV reboot, Clark and Lois meet on the job at the Daily Planet, and their love story is an integral part of the total narrative. When Superman’s not saving the world, Clark and Lois eventually find time to marry, but Clark’s super powers prevent the couple from conceiving a child. However, in the final season, a baby appears on screen, wrapped in Superman’s red cape.

Top photo: Matt Sayles/AP; Left: Warner Brothers

Tom Welling

TV series: 'Smallville'

Year(s): 2001 to 2011

During this teen-centric show, a young Clark Kent discovers his destiny and the superhuman powers attached to it. Growing up in Smallville, Clark experiences the growing pains of stepping into the heroic role of Superman, as well as the growing pains of teenage life.

Top photo: Jason Kempin/Getty; Left: Michael Courtney/AP

Brandon Routh

Movie: 'Superman Returns'

Year(s): 2006

Superman returns to Earth after a lengthy visit to his home planet of Krypton, where life had all but vanished. He once again faces off with Lex Luthor, who, in an act of vengeance, is planning to use Kryptonite against Superman in more ways than one and wreak havoc on the earth.

Top photo: Matt Sayles/AP; Left: David James/AP

Henry Cavill

Movie: 'Man of Steel'

Year(s): 2013 to present

A young Clark Kent slowly learns of his extraordinary powers and abilities in this new film. As he struggles with questions regarding why he is different and why he was sent to Earth from Krypton in the first place, the planet he now calls home (Earth) is invaded by aliens. To make matters worse, these enemies are members of his own race.
